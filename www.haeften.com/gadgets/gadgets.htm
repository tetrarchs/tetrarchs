Gadget diary

Dec 2007

Apple Mac mini core duo. My new desktop after my super-duper PC kept fatally crashing (after 3 years of continuous running). I have upgraded the memory from 1GB to 2GB and also bought a ½ terabyte USB drive to compensate for the somewhat measly 160GB drive included in the Mac mini. The extra drive helps with running the Leopard Time Machine so that I can go back in time and grab previous versions of any file.

 

Dec 2006

Sony HXD870. This came bundled with my Sony flatscreen TV. It has one digital tuner, has a DVDRW, a hard disk (180GB) and can "dub" to a DVD. Its main claim to fame is that it upscales the picture for HD compatibility, this seems to work pretty well and gets rid of some of the unwanted artifacts you sometimes get with DVDs on a HD screen.
The end result is that we now have 3 Freeview tuners connected to the TV (plus one in the TV), two 180GB PVR hard disks, the DVDRW, the amplifier, CD player (with 5 CD changer) and the old video cassette recorder (with analogue tuner).

 

Sony Bravia 40" KDL-40W2000U. My first LCD flat screen TV (to replace the old 37" CRT TV which went pop after exactly 5 years). This has 2 SCART inputs and 2 HDMI inputs as well as other useful inputs. Freeview tuner built in (useful if you only want to switch on the TV).
The screen is great quality and large enough so that I can enjoy the picture without having to wear my glasses all the time, I can recommend going for the extra couple of inches.

 

Oct 2005

Humax PVR9200T. My personal video recorder. It has 160GB hard disk space, enough for about 100 recordings, and was the first on the market with two digital tuners so that you can record and watch another channel or make two recordings at the same time or use the picture-in-picture function. You can transfer recordings via a USB2 socket to a PC but it's tricky to set up. Recordings can be scheduled on a programme guide. This works extremely well with freeview TV and has transformed our TV watching completely as we now watch recorded TV rather than live (primarily so we can skip the adverts).

 

Oct 2004

JumpDrive Trio. Single Slot Multi-Card Reader. This takes SD, Memory Stick, Memory Stick PRO, MMC and xD-Picture cards. Very useful for transferring photos from my phone when I'm out and about.

 

Sep 2004

Nokia 6320. Very much a working phone but with all the high tech features you could wish for. I get this phone free from work but I've found it a boon when commuting as with the addition of a 512 MB MMC memory card I can listen to music as well as the in-built radio.
The photos it takes seem okay and the camera has not significantly bulked up the phone.
Though you can play video on the phone this seems a rather weak gimmick unless you are desperate to watch you favorite film played at 128x128 resolution. I have watched an episode of Futurama on the phone and in fact cartoons are probably the best thing to play back.

 

Mar 2004

Sony CyberShot DSC-T1. Super little camera bought from pixmania to take on holiday and to keep with me around town. 5MP and 3x optical zoom, the camera is small enough to hide in the palm of your hand. Bought a 512K Sony duo memory stick to go with it which pushed up the price but could come in handy if I go away for a week without a laptop to download the images to. I even tried out the video capability at a wedding, surprisingly good result in a poorly lit room recording at 640x480.
The sensor is good is poor lighting but I normally need to touch up the photos in ACDSee to get best quality results. Sony do not supply a case but my zip up case for my Sony Ericsson T68i fits the camera perfectly.
Up to now I have used an Olympus 3030C but this is too bulky to carry all the time on the off chance of taking photos.

 

Jan 2004

Antec Sonata PC case for £90. As a little project I treated my PC to a new motherboard (ABIT IC7-G) and as I would need a new power supply, I went the whole hog and got a quiet PC case to go with it. Not a project for the faint-hearted, I've changed motherboards before but starting with a generic empty case was a fair challenge and took a day to assemble and a week to configure everything properly (not counting the time to get a replacement motherboard when the first one turned up with it's main fan broken off!).
My PC now brags 200 GB of disk space (over 3 hard disks), a processor running at 2.4 GHz and 1 GB of memory. The PC makes about 1/3 of the noise it used to in the old case.

 

Dec 2003

NAD C320BEE ordered from QED for £175. A treat before Christmas of this fantastic amplifier for our hifi system. My hifi is a mixed bag of equipment from different suppliers, this NAD amplifier brings them together beautifully.

 

Nov 2003

Sony Ericsson T68i ordered from Virgin mobile (my long term pay-as-you-go mobile phone account provider as they are sooo cheap) with my £20 current user discount, the phone cost £109 and came with an extra £10 voucher of Virgin airtime.

 

Oct 2003

Sony Freeview Digital TV Receiver (VTX-D800U). Lovely design and a neat menu system for the program guide. Cheapest price I could find was on Amazon, particularly due to their offer of free delivery for orders over £25.

 

June 2003

Habitat LED clock. This is exactly the same clock as Professor Ryan (Silent Witness) has on her desk. I use it in the bedroom as it´s a clock I can see in the dark without my glasses on!

Oct 2001

Yelo 0800 DVD Player. Some irritating niggles on usability but a useful player as I can record from DVD to my VCR as macrovision protection can be by-passed and the player is fully multi-region.

June 1999

Palm V. Reliable and useful gadget, bought for £250 for work purposes. Over the years I have changed the default diary to datebk3 and the contact book to TealPhone, both offering useful improvements. Of great help is Word Complete a great little application that guesses the word you are typing in after two letters. The most recent palmtops offer music and better connectivity but this old stalwart holds my diary, contacts, to-dos, train timetable, outlook email and notes so I am still waiting for a really good integrated mobile phone as an alternative (I have used a Sony Ericsson P800 (2003) but I found it a great disappointment).
