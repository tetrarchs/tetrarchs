Purpose

I have had several contacts randomly from other Van Haeftens, using email search tools, researching their genealogy. The intent of creating this page is to act as a focus for this information. I am particularly interested in building up my library of records and photographs to support the database and ensure the quality of the information.

Since I started this family web page, a book has been published on our family called “The Van Haeften family in India (1735-1948)” by Tom Fuller, which is based on records from the India Office.

Database

The database is off-line for a short while. I am experimenting with GenealogyJ as a free way to maintain the family tree. Genealogy for Java is based on open source code that enables the user to manage GEDCOM files in a graphical way regardless of the underlying operating system (I'm running it on a Mac Mini with the Leopard operating system).

The update means that I'm having to check through the current database and update an old GEDCOM file from a couple of years back. The long term advantage should be the ability to share the GEDCOM file as well as the charts from it.

The following baptism certificate shows my Grandfather Clement Alston Van Haeften's birth (1895) and marriage dates (1924, to Alice Gertrude D'Silva) and names my Great-Grandfather as James Michael Van Haeften and his Godfather as Albert George Van Haeften.

(2) Rachael Feather b.1884 d.1935

I have a certified copy of the death certificate for my Great-Grandmother, Rachael Feather (née Rachael Lewis) showing her dying on 31 January 1935 at age 51, the widow of William Feather (Tailor).  Rachael (Rose) may have been born as Rose Michelovsky.  It also known that William Feather had not died at that time but that’s a family story that I will not repeat here.

The family address is given as 66 Stanhope Street, Liverpool.  The death is registered by my Great-Uncle Ash Feather (who I am named after).
