Slavery in the Classical World: Week 6

 

SLAVE RESPONSES TO SLAVERY

 

Sources: Wiedemann chs. 10 and 11, plus no. 80 pp. 84-6; Yavetz 1988

The main sources for the Roman revolts are Diodorus Siculus Books 34 and 36, and Plutarch, Life of Crassus: what sort of problems are there in trying to use these sources?

Note: you will ALL be expected to have read the accounts of the revolts and be able to talk about them

 

Questions to think about:

How frequent were slave revolts in antiquity?  What governs their frequency?  Are they typical of certain times and/or places?  What factors are the principal causes of revolts?  Are there any common features in the character and progress of revolts? What aim or aims do such revolts have?

 

To what extent can comparative evidence from other slave societies be used to throw light on the revolts in antiquity?

 

How many other slaves responses to, or strategies for coping with, slavery can you identify?  Can slave responses/revolts be matched to those of any other groups in the ancient world?
