Slavery in the Classical World: Week 10

 

CHRISTIANITY AND SLAVERY

 

Sources:  Wiedemann ch. 12; Garnsey, Ideas of Slavery (1996) espec. chs. 10-14

Legal sources: Pharr, Theodosian Code (1952), Coleman-Norton, Roman State and Christian Church (1966); A. Linder, The Jews in Roman Imperial Legislation (1987) and The Jews in Legal Sources of the Early Middle Ages (1997)

 

Questions to consider:  What attitude or attitudes do early Christian writers take towards slavery?  To what extent do these derive from earlier thinkers?  What uses are made of slavery as part of Christian thought? Do you detect anything new in Christian thought in this area?  Can any changes in Christian thought be detected across the first centuries of the era?  Do Christian ideas have any impact on 1) imperial legislation on slavery, 2) actual treatment of slaves or patterns of slaveholding?
