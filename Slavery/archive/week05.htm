Slavery in the Classical World: Week 5

Part 1

TREATMENT OF SLAVES

 

Sources: Wiedemann, esp. ch. 9; Gardner/Wiedemann ch. 2;

 

Questions to think about:

How were slaves treated?  What motivated this treatment: e.g. fear, affection, economic or ideological reasons &c?  How did the the treatment of slaves differ from that of the free, and if so, how do these differences alter over time?  To what extent did slaves have any protection under either Athenian or Roman law?  How and when did these change, if at all, and for what reasons?  How effective were they?

 

What part did slaves play in the legal process and how was this affected by their slave status; e.g. as litigants, accused, witnesses?  What role did torture play under Athenian and Roman law?  How was is justified?

Part 2

SLAVES AND DRAMA

 

Choose and read at least ONE play by either Aristophanes or Plautus.  You must be prepared to talk about the play.

 

Consider the following questions:

What slave characters appear in the play?  How are they characterized, and is this characterization a stereotype?  What part is played in this by masks?  How important are they to the plot; e.g. incidental, crucial, main character etc.?  How  important is their slave-status to the plot?

 

What generally is said about slaves and slavery in the play?  Who can attend ancient performances?  Which audience, or part of the audience, is the dramatist addressing?

 

Can drama throw any light on the real life of slaves?  Does this matter?
