Slavery in the Classical World: Week 8

 

FREEDMEN AT ROME

 

Sources: Gardner/Wiedemann ch. 7

Petronius, Cena Trimalchionis from the Satyricon

On imperial freedmen: Tacitus, Annals esp. bks 11-13; Pliny, Letters 8.6; Suetonius, Life of Claudius; Statius, Silvae 3.3

On imperial eunuchs, see Claudian, In Eutropium, with Long 1996

 

EVERYONE to read the Cena, and be prepared to talk about it.  Think about the following: Who was Petronius?  When did he live?  What sort of work is he writing and for whom?  Does he have an ‘agenda’?

What light is thrown upon freedmen and slaves in Roman society?  Consider Trimalchio’s attitude to himself and his status; his career and wealth; his social milieu.

 

Wider questions:

What in general was the status of freedmen at Rome?  What restrictions/burdens did they carry compared to the freeborn?  What social stigma did they suffer, why and from whom? What sort of careers could they enjoy?  What degree of social mobility was open to them and their descendants?

Why were imperial freedmen so important?  What functions did they perform for the emperor?  Why did this cause problems, who objected? Think of the same in relation to eunuchs in later antiquity?
