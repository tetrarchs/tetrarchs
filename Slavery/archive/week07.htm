Slavery in the Classical World: Week 7

 

MANVMISSION and FREEDMEN AT ATHENS

MANVMISSION AT ROME

 

 

Sources: Wiedemann Sourcebook ch. 3 and Gardner/Wiedemann ch. 7

[Demosthenes], Oration 59 Against Neaera

 

Athens: EVERYONE to read Against Neaera, and be prepared to talk about it, for the light it throws of Athenian attitudes to male/female, slave/free, citizen/metic and anything else you can think of!

 

Rome: What are the methods and procedures for granting freedom to a slave?  How did the different methods effect the freed slave’s status? How frequently was freedom granted?  Who was most/least likely to be freed?  What reasons/motives might a slave owner have for granting freedom?
